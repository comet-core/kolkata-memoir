The heavy fog of the first forty-eight hours finally started to lift a little bit this morning. We were packing up to head out to Narendrapur—where my mom’s older sister lives—for some rituals at a nearby ashram. 

While waiting for the cab, the reality of splitting up hit. Going from a house packed with people down to just a few of us felt incredibly quiet. I missed the chaos of everyone back at Sodepur almost immediately, but reuniting with my aunt brought its own kind of comfort. It was a different, quieter anchor.

Later that day, we ventured out to Adi Dhakeshwari Bastralaya to buy traditional clothing for the *Shraddha* on June 3rd. Knowing exactly what this clothing was for added a weight to the trip, but it also felt good to do something relatively normal, just walking around a store and being distracted for the time being.

However, by the time we were sitting in the cab on the way back, the sheer exhaustion of the journey completely caught up to me. You don’t really realize how much your brain is processing until you finally get a moment to just sit still in traffic and zone out.
