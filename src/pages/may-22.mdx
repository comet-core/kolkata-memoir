The flight from Bombay was a blur. Two hours suspended in the air, watching the sun set through a scratched plastic window, knowing exactly what was waiting for us when we landed. You are moving at hundreds of miles an hour, but your mind is completely paralyzed.

Even though the city was wide awake at 22:00, the silence outside the building was deafening.

Trudging up the stairs, one heavy step at a time, the adrenaline of the journey finally wore off and the reality of why we were here settled in. I remember the brief, quiet exchanges on the staircase; nobody really had the words yet. My head was pounding, but you just keep moving because that is what you have to do.

The rest of the night moved in a blur of tradition and necessary steps. We carried her down. We made the slow trip to the banks of the Ganga. I stood there with my cousins, watching my uncles perform the rituals and then going to sit by the banks of the Ganges river night sky, catching up with each other. At that peaceful moment, it was just us, the wind, and the river.

You don't realize how loud a city is until you are forced to stand completely still in the middle of it.
