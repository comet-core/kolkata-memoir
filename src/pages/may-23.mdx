Waking up the next morning is always the strangest part. The adrenaline is completely gone, the house is full in a long time, and the reality of the framed photo with the white garland sits quietly in the room with you. 

There was no heightened drama today, and we didn't need there to be. The house just adapted to the weight of it all.

The day just went on. It was a day of slow movements and shared spaces. We went to visit one of her old friends who couldn't be there the night before. It is strange how you end up comforting other people while you are trying to process it yourself, but just sitting together helps.

The most comforting part of the day was just the sheer volume of us being crammed together. Eating breakfast, sitting on the beds, taking a photo just to capture the fact that we were all in one place. You don't have to talk about the sadness constantly when everyone in the room already understands it. Sometimes, just sitting shoulder-to-shoulder is enough.
