The morning was low-key. My younger sister, my mama, and I headed to Baromandir Ghat (which translates to 12 Temples Ghat, though I definitely didn't count twelve). The goal was to find a barber for the upcoming *Khourokarmo* rituals on June 2nd. Aside from some very photogenic pigeons, it was a quiet, practical start to the day.

The afternoon was a completely different story. We made a plan with exactly one destination in mind: Park Street. 

It was a full squad deployment — me, my mom, both my sisters, my mama, and my mami. We left the house around 3:20 PM and took autos to Dunlop. My mama and I shared one, while the ladies took another, and we all converged at the Baranagar Road station to catch the metro.

Taking the Kolkata Metro was an experience. It had been a *very* long time since I'd been on it, so it felt like a first. Walking onto the platform, I immediately noticed the lack of overhead power cables — they use a third-rail system here instead of the overhead wires we have in Mumbai. 

Stepping inside the train was a physical shock. The AC was absolutely blasting, creating an intense microclimate compared to the brutal Kolkata heat outside.

The 35-minute ride to Park Street was full of minor engineering mysteries. Shortly before Dum Dum station, the train just randomly lost power for five seconds before flicking back on. No one else seemed to notice or care, so I just watched the locals act completely normal while the lights tripped. After Dum Dum, we went entirely underground, and the station platforms started switching sides seemingly at random, keeping me guessing the whole way down.

When we finally emerged at Park Street station, the vibe shifted immediately.

We started walking toward the legendary Peter Cat. But before we could even get there, we crossed paths with a guy selling proper street-side Masala Chai. Naturally, we had to stop. Not everyone wanted one, so we grabbed four cups, stood on the pavement, and took in the chaos of the street.
