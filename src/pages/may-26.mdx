The transition back to Sodepur was gradual. Long before we packed our bags, the day began in complete quiet. While I was still dead asleep, my mom and her sister headed out to a local mandir for some early morning rituals—a peaceful, grounding start before the journey back.

Before facing the drive, my mom and I took a detour to South City Mall. After days wrapped in a quiet, emotionally heavy bubble, stepping out into the everyday buzz of the city felt like stepping onto another planet. Seeing the sheer scale of the high-rises outside was awe-inspiring, a stark reminder of the world carrying on. 

We spent a while just mindlessly wandering through the air-conditioned crowds before ending up at the food court. We took some goofy photos with a statue, and I grabbed Domino's—because no matter what's going on, you can't go wrong with pizza. It was the perfect, simple reset.

Around 3:30 PM, we finally hit the road, pulling into Sodepur just over an hour later. I had braced myself to walk back into the same packed, chaotic house we had left behind, but it was surprisingly still. The intense, suffocating weight of the first few days had evaporated. The house had finally settled into a much lighter, easier rhythm.

By 10:00 PM, a few of us were just lounging in the AC room, winding down for the night. You could physically feel the mood shifting; the fun was finally allowed to creep back in. We spent the evening just talking, catching up on the last two days, and simply existing around each other without the heavy silence hanging over our heads.
