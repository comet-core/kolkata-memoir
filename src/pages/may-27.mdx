The lighter rhythm we had settled into the night before carried right over into the morning. Around 9:00 AM, my mom, my younger sister, her dad, and I headed out for breakfast. 

We were just walking across the street to a restaurant called Shibani, but even a short walk in the morning heat was an event. The society compound was quiet, and the sun was already absolutely brutal.

Breakfast at Shibani was daal kachori, and it was honestly as good as it gets. It is funny how something as simple as grabbing street food with family can completely shift the energy of a day. 

The walk back was essentially just us messing around. We spent half the time treating my mom like a celebrity, taking pictures of her every time she moved. I also apparently adopted the role of the intimidating family bodyguard.

Later that evening, the tone of the day shifted entirely. We went over to my grandmother's old apartment. 

Stepping inside felt like walking through a portal. The familiar smell of the place hit me instantly, bringing back an avalanche of childhood memories. Every time we visited Kolkata when I was younger, this was where we stayed. The walls had started to crack a bit, but the essence of the house was exactly the same.

It was a harsh reality check. You want things to be exactly how they were, but you know they never can be again. 

Going out onto the balcony made my throat feel heavy. That used to be my absolute favorite place to hang out. I spent some time just standing out there, taking photos of the fading sky and watching the street below, trying to soak it all in one last time.
