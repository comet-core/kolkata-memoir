The lighter rhythm we had settled into the night before carried right over into the morning. Around 9:00 AM, my mom, my younger sister, her dad, and I headed out for breakfast at a place across the street called Shibani. 

The short walk there was essentially just us messing around. We spent half the time treating my mom like a celebrity, taking pictures of her every time she moved. Meanwhile, I was caught on camera looking like a very unconvincing bouncer.

Breakfast at Shibani was daal kachori, and it was honestly as good as it gets. It is funny how something as simple as grabbing street food with family can completely shift the energy of a day. 

On the walk back, we took it slow and sat for a bit by the pond inside the society compound. It was a beautiful, quiet spot to just relax, though the peace was briefly interrupted by the immediate aftermath of me trying Hajmola.

Later that evening, we went over to my grandmother's old apartment. 

Stepping inside felt like walking through a portal. The familiar smell of the place hit me instantly, bringing back an avalanche of childhood memories. Every time we visited Kolkata when I was younger, this was where we stayed. The walls had started to crack a bit, but the essence of the house was exactly the same.

It was a harsh reality check. You want things to be exactly how they were, but you know they never can be again. 

Going out onto the balcony now did not feel the same as it used to. That used to be my absolute favorite place to hang out. I spent some time just standing out there, taking photos of the fading sky and watching the street below, trying to soak it all in one last time.
