Today was the core reason we came out to Narendrapur. My mom and my aunt sat through a long *puja* at the local ashram, centered around my grandmother's memory. It was a quiet, heavy morning, and I spent most of it just sitting nearby, being present with them while the rituals took place.

As solemn as the day was, there was a lot of warmth to it. Around nineteen of my aunt’s friends showed up just to be there for them. They sat together and sang, and seeing a community physically show up to wrap around your family makes the weight of it all a little easier to carry. 

Afterward, we all sat down for lunch at the ashram. It was simple, incredibly good vegetarian food. Sometimes, a quiet, shared meal after a heavy event is exactly what everyone needs to ground themselves.

By the time we finally got back to the house and the evening rolled around, the sheer exhaustion of the day had settled. I went up to the rooftop just to get some air and escape for a bit. 

Going from a crowded ashram to a completely silent roof is jarring in the best way possible. I spent a while up there just taking photos of the flowers, watching the sun go down over the neighborhood, and zooming in on Venus and Jupiter in the night sky. A much-needed moment to just breathe and look at something else.
